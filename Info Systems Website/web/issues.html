The issues

The perceived issues concerning the ability that P2P file sharing has, to freely, easily and securely facilitate the transfer of files between computers, largely revolve around the idea of intellectual property and the owners of that IP being able to profit from their work. Looking at P2P file sharing through the most basic lens logic would dictate that if people have the ability to download media for free which they would otherwise pay for, they would choose the former and it would therefore have a significant negative impact on the creator of the content and the industry as a whole. And while this is the view that most law makers hold, it is not a view grounded in fact, because it does not account for factors such as the varying experiences you get from media the accessibility of P2P file sharing and the type of people who do it and why they do it.
